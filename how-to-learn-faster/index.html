How to learn faster?

There are two modes of the human brain

The two modes are the most efficient to use alternately. If you knock yourself to death in any mode, you are doing useless work.

Focused mode: your brain is mainly learning, entering information, and doing information matching work. It is usually started when we are working and studying.
Diffuse mode: your brain is doing internal analysis, generating new links, re-linking and kneading old knowledge. This state is initiated when we sleep, exercise and other relaxed states. Sometimes you have inspiration when you take a shower, and that's what this pattern does.

 To sum up, it is necessary to stop and play after studying for a period of time. It's not laziness, but only in this way, your brain works most efficiently.

Procrastination is normal

According to scientists, when we encounter something we don't like or something very difficult, the brain secretes a special substance that makes you feel uncomfortable. Then it naturally encourages you to focus on other things, and you procrastinate. 

So, first of all, you have to know that procrastination is a normal human behavior, so don't blame yourself too much. 

What are we going to do with it? Our brains also have a magical function, that is, once you focus on a task for more than five minutes, this particular substance will stop secreting. So the solution to doing unhappy things is to force yourself to start for five minutes, and then everything will be fine.

About memory. 

The content needs to be repeated constantly, and the higher the previous repetition frequency, the longer the memory will be preserved

Which of the following ways can achieve a better and faster memory effect if you want to remember a poem?
A memorize over 20 times a day.
B recite 10 times on the first day, 5 times on the second day, and 5 times on the 4th day.
The answer is B. Because memory is divided into short-term memory and long-term memory, the way to convert short-term memory into long-term memory is to repeat over a period of time, rather than one at a time.

Sleep is a magic weapon that can make you smarter

What are our brains doing while we sleep?
1. Clean up the garbage secretions produced by heavy exercise during the day.
2. Reorganize the information entered during the day, classify them and re-link them. Suppose the brain is a large cabinet with many drawers, and the large amount of information entered during the day is like all kinds of things that have just been bought. After going to bed at night, the brain classifies these things into various drawers one by one, so that you can use them later.
3. Strengthen the link between new knowledge and old knowledge, and subconsciously keep thinking about the problems that have not been solved during the day. The most important thing is that these movements can only be done during sleep, so sleep is really an important task.

To build a strong framework, you need two things, Chunking and Connecting

Chunking, the knowledge we take in every day, like building bricks in a house, is independent. If you want to build a house, you must have enough bricks, so it is necessary to learn all kinds of information. To build a house, you also need cement to connect these bricks. This requires that every time you learn a knowledge, you should think about the causes and consequences of this knowledge, what are the rules, and so on.
Connecting, you will have the ability to innovate. Because you can find a pattern in the connection of each small module. Then as you have more and more connections and become more and more powerful, there will be more possibilities for connections. The ability to innovate is coming.

What to do when you need to learn something difficult

First, if you need to learn something that is difficult,, don't focus on the results, enjoy the process. Paying too much attention to the result will make you give up.
The second is to give yourself periodic rewards, which is very important. Proper self-discipline is freedom, and excessive self-discipline is self-harm. Your respect for human nature, respect for the nature of your brain, and a reward from time to time will make it easier for you to stick to it. The interval between rewards should not be too long. For example, if you lose weight for 5 days, you can eat freely; after 2 hours of homework, you can play with your cell phone for 10 minutes. After this reward, you are full of energy.
Third, believe in yourself and don't blame yourself all the time. As long as this time is a little better than last time, it will be all right. Of course, if it's not good once in a while, it doesn't matter, it's better next time.
